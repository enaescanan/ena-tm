One of the leading causes of
maternal deaths in the Philippines are complications due to hypertension.

Other causes are postpartum hemorrhage and abortive outcome.

On average, 4-5 women die per day from pregnancy or childbirth-related complications from 1991 to 2010.

Hypertension is a long-term medical condition in which the blood pressure
in your arteries is persistently elevated. This puts you at risk for heart disease and stroke.

A contributor to developing this condition is having certain medical conditions like diabetes but unhealthy behaviors like smoking, eating food high in sodium, drinking too much alcohol, and being obese can also increase your risk for high blood pressure.
